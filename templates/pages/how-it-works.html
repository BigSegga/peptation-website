Understanding Hair Loss

Why Your Hair Is Thinning

Hair loss isn't random. It follows a predictable biological pattern driven by one primary culprit: DHT (Dihydrotestosterone).

DHT is created when an enzyme called 5-alpha reductase converts testosterone into its more potent form. While DHT is important for development, it becomes destructive when it accumulates in hair follicles.

When DHT binds to receptors in your follicles, it triggers follicular miniaturization — your follicles progressively shrink, producing thinner, weaker hair until they stop producing altogether.

This is why most treatments fail. They address symptoms without targeting the underlying biological mechanisms. Peptation takes a different approach.

Meet GHK-Cu

GHK-Cu (Glycyl-L-histidyl-L-lysine copper) is a naturally occurring copper peptide found in human blood plasma. It's not a drug — it's a compound your body already produces, just in smaller quantities as you age.

Research shows GHK-Cu levels decline significantly after age 20. By reintroducing this peptide topically, we can reactivate the biological processes that promote healthy hair growth.
